Revenue is the total income generated from business operations before deducting any expenses. It represents the top-line performance of a company.

Net profit is calculated as revenue minus all operating expenses, taxes, interest, and other costs. It reflects the true profitability of a company.

Gross profit is revenue minus the cost of goods sold (COGS). It measures how efficiently a company produces or sources its products.

Operating income is the profit earned from core business operations excluding taxes and interest expenses.

Customer acquisition cost (CAC) is the total marketing and sales expense required to acquire a new customer.

Lifetime value (LTV) represents the total revenue a business expects from a customer throughout their relationship.

Churn rate measures the percentage of customers who stop using a company's product or service during a given period.

Market share represents the percentage of total industry sales earned by a company.

Return on investment (ROI) measures the profitability of an investment relative to its cost.

Break-even point is the level of sales at which total revenue equals total expenses.

Cash flow refers to the movement of money into and out of a business. Positive cash flow indicates more money entering than leaving.

Accounts receivable represents money owed to a company by customers for goods or services delivered but not yet paid.

Accounts payable represents money a company owes to suppliers for products or services received.

Inventory turnover measures how often inventory is sold and replaced during a period.

Operating margin is calculated as operating income divided by revenue. It indicates operational efficiency.

A balance sheet provides a snapshot of a company's assets, liabilities, and shareholders' equity at a specific point in time.

An income statement shows revenues, expenses, and profits over a period.

Working capital is calculated as current assets minus current liabilities.

Data engineering involves designing, building, and maintaining data pipelines to ensure reliable data flow.

ETL stands for Extract, Transform, Load, and it refers to the process of collecting data, cleaning it, and storing it in a structured format.

Data warehouses store structured data optimized for analytical queries.

Data lakes store raw structured and unstructured data in its native format.

A fact table contains quantitative data for analysis, while dimension tables contain descriptive attributes related to facts.

Batch processing handles large volumes of data at scheduled intervals.

Real-time processing handles data immediately as it is generated.

Data validation ensures data quality by checking accuracy, completeness, and consistency.

Observability in data systems refers to monitoring pipelines, detecting failures, and ensuring reliability.

Vector databases store embeddings and allow semantic similarity search.

Embeddings convert text into numerical vectors that capture semantic meaning.

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) combines document retrieval with language model generation to produce context-aware responses.

Large Language Models (LLMs) generate text based on patterns learned during training.

Chunking is the process of splitting large documents into smaller segments for embedding and retrieval.

Metadata provides additional contextual information about data such as timestamps or categories.

Semantic search finds documents based on meaning rather than exact keyword matches.
